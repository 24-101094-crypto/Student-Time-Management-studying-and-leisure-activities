In this project, I analyzed how students balance studying and leisure activities in their daily lives and explored how this balance may relate to stress levels and overall satisfaction. The goal of this analysis was to better understand students’ time management habits and identify whether leisure activities tend to interfere with academic responsibilities.

For this project, I worked with survey data collected from 60 student responses. I cleaned, processed, and analyzed the dataset using Python in Google Colab. The analysis included descriptive statistics, data visualization, and pattern exploration to better interpret the survey results.

Throughout the project, I used libraries such as Pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib, and Seaborn to organize the data, calculate statistical measures, and create visual representations of the findings. I examined factors such as daily leisure hours, perceived balance between studying and leisure, academic stress, and satisfaction with current time management habits.

The findings suggest that many students spend a significant amount of time on leisure activities, which may contribute to increased stress levels and lower satisfaction when balance is not effectively managed. This project demonstrates how data analysis can be used to explore behavioral trends and gain meaningful insights from survey-based datasets
